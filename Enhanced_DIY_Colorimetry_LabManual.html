Part C: Building Your DIY Colorimeter Box

1
Select your box: Choose a cardboard box that can fit your cuvette or test tube standing upright inside. The box should be approximately 15-20 cm long.
2
Cut the front viewing hole: On the front panel (where your phone camera will be positioned), cut a rectangular opening approximately 5-8 cm wide and 5-8 cm tall, centered on the panel. This is where your phone camera will "see" through to the sample.
3
Remove the back panel: Cut out the ENTIRE back panel of the box. This allows ambient light to illuminate the colored construction paper that will serve as your light source background.
4
Position the construction paper: Tape a sheet of GREEN construction paper flat behind the open back of the box. The paper should be fully visible through the front viewing hole when you look through the sample position. Keep it flat and wrinkle-free.
5
Secure and stabilize: Place the box on a stable surface (use a book for elevation if needed so the viewing hole is at phone-camera height). Tape the box down securely — nothing should move between measurements!
6
Check your lighting: Use steady room light (dim overhead lights if too bright). Avoid direct sunlight as it causes fluctuating readings. The light path is: room light → reflects off green paper → passes through sample → exits front hole → enters phone camera.
7
Test your RGB app: Open your color analyzer app and place your phone camera flush against the front viewing hole. If your app allows, lock the exposure and focus (tap-and-hold usually works). Place a blank (water) in the sample position — the green paper should appear bright in the green channel.
Pro Tip: Consistency is everything! Mark the exact position where you place the cuvette/tube. Every sample must go in the SAME spot with the SAME orientation.

Part E: Data Collection

1
Select your measurement channel: Test with your blank (water) and concentrated standard (100%). Determine which RGB channel shows the LARGEST drop in intensity from blank to concentrated. For grape juice with green paper, the GREEN channel should drop most significantly.

Channel selected:

2
Measure the blank (I₀): Place your blank solution (0% grape juice = water) in the colorimeter box at the marked sample position. Close any light gaps, wait for stable reading, and record the intensity for your chosen channel. This is your I₀ value.
3
Measure standards from LOW to HIGH: Starting with 20%, measure each standard solution. Insert the cuvette in the same position/orientation every time, wait for stable reading, and record the intensity (I). Take 2-3 readings and calculate the average.
4
Calculate T and A: For each measurement, immediately calculate the transmittance and absorbance and record in your data table.
5
Measure the unknown: Following the same procedure, measure your unknown sample and record all values.
Transmittance: T = I / I₀    |    Absorbance: A = –log₁₀(T)
⚠️ Critical: Do NOT change the lighting, box position, or camera settings between measurements. Consistency is essential for accurate results!
